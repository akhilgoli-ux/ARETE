# Story Arc / Launch Sequence

Use this when building toward a specific moment — a waitlist push, a feature reveal,
a milestone, or a campaign. A story arc is a multi-week narrative where each week's
content builds on the last, so followers who stick around feel like they're part of something.

## Command
```
/social-ideas arc "[goal or moment]" --weeks N --instagram N/week --tiktok N/week
```

Examples:
```
/social-ideas arc "waitlist launch push" --weeks 4 --instagram 3/week --tiktok 5/week
/social-ideas arc "momentum meter reveal" --weeks 2 --instagram 4/week --tiktok 4/week
/social-ideas arc "constellation feature announcement" --weeks 3 --tiktok 5/week
```

---

## The 4-Week Launch Arc (standard)

This is the proven structure for a pre-launch or feature campaign.
Adapt the number of weeks based on how much time you have.

### WEEK 1 — PROBLEM AWARENESS
Goal: Make the audience feel the pain without mentioning the solution.
Tone: Empathetic. Observational. "You're not alone."

Content pillars to hit this week:
- The thing people don't track that matters most
- What it feels like to be "busy" but not intentional
- The gap between who you are and who you said you'd be
- Vulnerability post: something the founder/team noticed about their own life

Formats that work best: ugc-rant, vulnerability-caption, trojan-horse, quote-drop
CTA this week: None or very soft ("does this resonate?")

---

### WEEK 2 — AGITATION
Goal: Deepen the stakes. Show the cost of not solving the problem.
Tone: Honest. Slightly uncomfortable. "Here's what you're actually losing."

Content pillars to hit this week:
- What untracked habits cost over 12 months (compounding loss)
- The life areas most people sacrifice first (family, sleep, relationships)
- The data angle: people who track live differently than those who don't
- The ancient Greek connection: ἀρετή as a 2,400-year-old answer to this exact problem

Formats that work best: did-you-know, carousel (list format), countdown, before-after
CTA this week: "Something is coming. Follow to see it first."

---

### WEEK 3 — SOLUTION HINT / BUILD ANTICIPATION
Goal: Tease the solution without fully revealing it. Create FOMO.
Tone: Mysterious. Confident. "We built the answer."

Content pillars to hit this week:
- Behind the scenes of building ARETE (founder-story format)
- The constellation concept introduced (without fully explaining it)
- The momentum meter teased (what does a score for your life look like?)
- AI agents concept: "What if something watched everything for you?"

Formats that work best: founder-story, aesthetic-mood, voiceover-broll, pov
CTA this week: "Waitlist is opening soon. Link in bio."

---

### WEEK 4 — REVEAL / PUSH
Goal: Full reveal. Drive waitlist signups. Create urgency without desperation.
Tone: Exciting but still philosophical. "This is what we built."

Content pillars to hit this week:
- Full ARETE reveal: what it is, what it does, the constellation, the momentum meter
- Social proof if available: early testers, founding team using it
- The philosophical case: ARETE as ἀρετή made actionable
- Direct invitation: "You've been watching. Now join."

Formats that work best: did-you-know (features), carousel (full product intro),
                         ugc-rant (authentic reaction), quote-drop (philosophical sendoff)
CTA this week: "Join the waitlist. Link in bio." — every single post.

---

## Shorter Arc: 2-Week Version

Week 1 = compress Problem + Agitation
Week 2 = compress Solution Hint + Reveal

Use this for feature announcements (momentum meter, constellation) rather than full launches.
